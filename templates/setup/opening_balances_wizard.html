Opening Balances Guide
What are Opening Balances?

These are the starting amounts in your accounts at the beginning of your financial year. They represent your business position on day one.

Balance Equation:
Assets = Liabilities + Equity
This equation must always balance
Common Examples:
  • Cash: Bank account balances
  • Accounts Receivable: Money owed to you
  • Accounts Payable: Money you owe
  • Equipment: Value of business assets
  • Owner's Equity: Your investment
Tips:
  • Use your last financial statement
  • Check with your accountant
  • Assets and Equity are positive
  • Liabilities are positive amounts
  • Leave zero balances empty
Need Help?
Don't worry if you're not sure about some balances. You can always adjust them later.
